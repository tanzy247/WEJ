Hong Kong felt like an Asian London - they even had the red double decker buses! We enjoyed visiting local markets, parks and temples.

Zhangjiajie

Our next stop was Zhangjiajie - the setting of the Avatar movie. We enjoyed 2 days here. Visiting the glass bridge over the canyon and hiking through the national park. We also spent an evening watching a local show, which told Chinese stories. The acrobatics included in the show were incredible!

Xi’an

The Terracotta Warriors were the reason for our visit to Xi’an and they did not disappoint. The sheer size of the structure and underground kingdom is extremely impressive and to think it was built thousands of years ago is unbelievable. However, the efforts that the architects have and continue to go to is also just as impressive. Every single warrior has been put back together piece by piece and they continue to excavate and match up every individual piece.

Beijing

Our final stop in China was in the capital. Here we enjoyed a day sightseeing including visiting the Hutongs, temples, and the Forbidden City. We also enjoyed watching locals play what looked like badminton but with their feet. We also got to try the familiar duck pancakes, which you can only get in Beijing in China.

Beijing

We also visited The Great Wall which was a deserving wonder of the world. We first visited a section, which was extremely popular with tourists. Although impressive, it was overshadowed by the amount of people. We did our research and travelled out of our way via public transport to a different section, where we were able to get undisturbed views. It was definitely worth our time! Here we could truly appreciate the incredible structure. Similar to the warriors, it is hard to believe how something was built such a long time ago.

Overall we had a fantastic visit in China and would definitely recommend it.
