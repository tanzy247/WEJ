Vietnam

Ho Chi Minh

Cu Chi Tunnels

Ho Chi Minh

On the way to the tunnels we stopped off at a workshop where people make art by hand using egg shells. 50% of the profits go to people with disabilities due to the affects from agent orange from the Vietnamese war. It was impressive to see the hard work and the whole process that goes into make each individual piece.

Cu Chi Tunnels

It was interesting to visit the tunnels and discover the scale of the operation. It is unbelievable to imagine that the Viet Cong lived in the tunnels for such a long period of time. The tunnels, including all of the traps, show the initiative of the people.

Mekong Delta

Mekong Delta

First we took a relaxing boat ride down the river through the mangroves, before visiting a few islands to see where they produce different products such as honey, popcorn, coconut candy and bamboo cutlery.

War Remnants Museum

War Remnants Museum

The First Indochina War and the Vietnam War are the focus of this museum.

Na Trang

Na Trang

After a busy few weeks, we hopped onboard a sleeper bus and headed to Nha Trang. Here we spent a day relaxing.

Da Nang

Da Nang

After Nha Trang, we boarded another sleeper bus and headed to Da Nang.

Here we visited some of the main spots including dragon bridge, Han Market and Bac My A Market- famous for avocado ice cream (something I wonâ€™t be trying again anytime soon).

Hoi An

Hoi An

Hoi An was my favourite city to visit in Vietnam. We really enjoyed strolling round the streets in the Old Town. We also had a go on the famous coconut boats. After a quick spin, the locals rowed us down the river to a spot where we went crabbing. Sadly, we only managed to catch 1 tiny crab.

Unlike the guy next to us who caught 2 big ones!

Evening

In the evening we took part in a group cooking class which was absolutely fantastic. The ingredients were so fresh, and the food was absolutely delicious - one of our favourite meals in Vietnam. We made spring rolls, Vietnamese pancakes, papaya salad, lemongrass and chilli chicken and morning glory.

After enjoying our tasty meal, we finished the day off with a stroll along the river.

Hai Van Pass

Marble Mountains

Marble Mountains

The next day we jumped on the back of a bike and headed on a day trip, taking us further up the coast over Hai Van Pass.

Our first stop was Marble Mountains. These are five marble and limestone hills named after the five elements.

Coast Ride

Coast Ride

After Marble Mountains, We jumped back on the bikes making our way up the coast, stopping to see fisherman returning with a load of shrimp to sell.

Monkey Mountain

Monkey Mountain

Our next stop was Monkey Mountain - home to over 100 endangered species.

Hai Van Pass

Hai Van Pass

Our day on one of Vietnam's most famous roads concluded with more beautiful coastal views.

All Aboard!

After a busy day on the bikes we arrived in Hue in time to board our overnight train to Ninh Bimh. This was definitely our most luxury transport so far!

Ninh Binh

Trang An

Trang An

On our first day in Ninh Binh we enjoyed cycling around Trang An. We spent the afternoon cruising down the river, enjoying the gorgeous scenery and walked up to a viewpoint to overlook the river.

Tam Coc

Tam Coc

On our second day we were in Tam Coc. We hired a scooter to explore further out and enjoyed visiting different religious temples.

Mai Cau

Mai Cau

We really enjoyed our time out of the city so decided to head to Mai Cau - another area in the mountains. We spent two days here, visiting caves, waterfalls and rice fields.
