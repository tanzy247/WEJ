Nepal

Kathmandu

Kathmandu

We spent 2 days exploring Kathmandu. Visiting Kathmandu’s Old Town - Durbar Square and Kathmandu Valley - Bhaktapur. One evening we took part in a cooking class, which we thoroughly enjoyed and the food was absolutely delicious!

We also visited Swoyambhu Mahachaitya - a famous stupa with views over looking Kathmandu.

Pokhara

Day 1

Pokhara

After a bumpy bus ride from Kathmandu we arrived in Pokhara and enjoyed a sunset dinner but Phewa Lake.

The next day we heading up Sarangkot Mountain to go paragliding.

The scariest part was having to run off the mountain, but as soon as you were up in the air is was an enjoyable ride with some views of the city.

Unfortunately, the air pollution is currently really bad due to the lack of rain, so there were no views of the Annapurna region. However, we knew we would soon be hiking in the mountains so we didn’t mind.

Day 2

Day 2

We had a short boat ride on Phewa Lake and walked up to one of the eight World Peace Pagoda’s - Shanti Stupa.

Annapurna Circuit

Day 1

Day 1

Before starting our trek, we had to take a busy and bumpy bus ride followed by a jeep, before we finally arrived at the start of the trek.

We trekked through a handful of small villages where we got a glimpse into the local life.

Day 2

Day 2

We trekked through beautiful forest, along the valley and got our first tiny glimpse of the snow capped mountains. We stopped off at an apple orchard where we sampled the fresh juice and fruit which was absolutely delicious.

I have so much admiration for the porters. I wouldn’t have been able to do the trek without them, so I thought it was only fair to try and help them out… for 30 seconds!

Day 3

Day 3

Sadly, it was still cloudy so we still didn’t have any proper views of the mountains yet, but we passed a beautiful lake which perfectly mirrored it’s surroundings.

Day 4

Day 4

We continued the trek heading for Tilicho Lake Base Camp. Today we were teased with glimpses of views; however, we were desperate for the clouds to blow over so we could get a full view.

Day 5

Day 5

It was an early 4am start as we headed for Tilicho Lake. Sadly, it was the cloudiest day yet and we could barely see 5 metres in front of us.

However, by the time we got down and had breakfast, it was like a new day. Hallelujah! We could finally see the famous Himalayan mountains!

Day 6

Day 6

WOOHOO! We were relieved to wake to beautiful blue skies, where we enjoyed views of the mountains all day.

Day 7

Day 7

We continued our trek to High Camp - where we were fortunate to have more great views of the mountains.

Day 8

Day 8

It was another early start to reach the highest point, but we were relieved to wake to beautiful views.

I was so relieved to reach the top that I treated myself to a Diary Milk and cup of tea. It was heavenly!

Day 9

Day 9

We continued our trek to a small local village where we stayed the night in a homestay.

Day 10

Day 10

For our final early start we headed up on hill for sunrise. It started a little cloudy, and we were unsure if we would get much of a view, but thankfully the clouds lifted and we ended the trek on a high.

Bardiya National Park

Day 1

Day 1

We explored the village by jeep and watched the sunset.

Day 2

Day 2

We went on a 12 hour jeep safari hoping to see either elephants, rhinos or tigers. We knew that this is extremely difficult as they are completely wild but we were ecstatic to see a group of elephants as well as a lone male during our day. We were also lucky enough to see a tiger from a far with binoculars, which Mike was super excited about, since big cats are his favourite.

Day 3

Day 3



We set off at 6:30am for another 12 hour safari, this time walking. By 9am we had received a call from another guide that 2 tigers had been heard at a certain spot so we ran for 30 minutes hoping to reach them in time.

I was worried that we would miss them... however... we spent 7 hours sitting in the same spot, waiting for 2 tigers which we could hear mating in the grass. Time seemed to have slowed down. We were desperate to see them, but it had got to the point we questioned whether we had made the right decision to sit and wait.

Especially when we spent this time sitting in a tree. It certainly wasn’t the most comfortable.

Until BINGO! They crossed the river. INCREDIBLE.

And then something even more remarkable happened. A rhino appeared and chased the tigers.

We sat in the trees watching the rhino bathe in the river hoping to see if the tigers returned...

It was certainly our lucky day! One of tigers came back to the water for a drink. WHAT A DAY!

Waiting 7 hours in 40 degree heat seemed ludicrous but boy was it worth it. As the sun started to set we headed back to our accommodation. We couldn’t believe our luck!
