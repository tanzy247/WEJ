Vietnam

Hanoi

Hanoi

Train street is a famous row of coffee shops alongside a train track in Hanoi Old Quarter. Here you can enjoy the bustling atmosphere of the city and occasionally a train will pass.

We enjoyed 2 days exploring Hanoi, trying local delicacies including the egg coffee. We visited Jade Mountain temple, as well as St Josephâ€™s Cathedral. In the evening we watched a performance at the Water Puppet Theatre before enjoying a stroll around the night market.

You can get almost anything at the market or around the Old Quarter. Mike even got his shoe sewn up at the side of the road!

Sapa

Day 1

Day 1

Day 1 in Sapa we visited nearby Cat Cat Village. Here we learnt how the traditional clothing of the Black Hmongs are made using the marijuana plant.

We also got to watch a short traditional dance.

Day 2

Day 2

Day 2 we started our trek to another village where we would be staying the night. Unfortunately, the day started very cloudy; however, luckily in the afternoon, it cleared up slightly and we were able to get some views of the rice fields and villages.

En route through different villages, It was really nice to see the local people living their normal lives, farming, weaving, cooking etc.

At the end of a busy day trekking we enjoyed an egg hot chocolate which was very tasty!

Day 3

Day 3

Luckily the next day was much clearer and we had glorious sunshine, which showed off the beautiful landscape. Today we trekked through the bamboo forest into the mountains overlooking the valley.

Day 4

Day 4

On our final day in Sapa we trekked up Dragon Mountain overlooking Sapa Town and Mount Fansipan - Indochina's largest mountain.
