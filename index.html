Cambodia

Siem Reap

Angkor Wat

Temple

We had an early start for our first morning in Siem Reap. We arrived at the famous Angkor Wat temple for sunrise. Angkor Wat is a world heritage site and is the largest religious premises in the world.

Ta Prohm

Ta Prohm

We also visited a second temple nicknamed ‘tomb raider’ as the site was featured in the Angelina Jolie film. This was extremely impressive as to how it has been overtaken by nature after it was previously abandoned.

Pub Street

Evening

After a busy day, we spent the evening at Julie and Yorkie’s favourite street! We even took to trying a local cuisine - frogs.

Day 2

Day 2

On our second day in Siem Reap, we visited local hot spots including a stall where Palm Cakes are made. Here we were able to see how they use palm fruits to make candy, sugar, and delicious cakes - using every single last bit of the palm. It was extremely impressive and admirable to see how hard they all work.

Kulen Mountain

Mountain

Our second stop was to Kulen mountain. Here we visited a waterfall, which locals believe to be holy water where they visit to cleanse themselves.

Reclining Buddha

Temple

After, we visited a temple containing the reclining Buddha, where we saw lots of locals leaving offerings.

Floating Villages

Afternoon

In the afternoon we visited the floating villages.

Battambang

Bamboo Train

On our first trip in Battambang we visited the bamboo train - a train made from bamboo which was previously completely operated by hand. Today they have been adapted to include small engines; however, the carriage itself is still made from bamboo and can be easily removed from the track. This was absolutely incredible to see when we were asked to step off the track mid-journey, where they removed the carts by hand so a ‘real’ modern train could come through.

We even crossed a highway!

Killing Cave and Temple

Afternoon

After the bamboo train, we visited the killing cave where thousands of Cambodians were killed during the genocide.

We then visited the temple.

We ended the day watching the sunset at bat cave. This is where millions of bats suddenly fly out of the cave, and head to find food before they return in the morning at sunrise.

Day 2

Day 2

We went on a fantastic trip with a local tuk tuk driver who had grown up on a farm. He worked on his family farm from the age of 8-21 and then moved to the city. His family still own and work on the farm including his mother who worked everyday in the field up and until her late 70’s! She is now 84 and still healthy and active - absolutely incredible!

Our driver took us to different stops in the morning to learn how the local area make a living.

This included stopping for breakfast at a local stall where they make sticky rice. They cook the rice inside bamboo and start the whole process themselves from scratch. Using coconuts to add flavour to the rice - it was absolutely delicious!

Phnom Penh

Day 1

Killing Fields and Prison

Our first day in Phnom Penh was extremely sombre as we learnt more about how the country was affected by genocide. It was absolutely horrendous to hear how the people were treated and brutally murdered. At one prison where over 18,000 people were killed, 12 survived. 2 of which are still alive and we were honoured to meet.

Evening

Evening

That evening we were visited by some hornbills at the rooftop of our hotel, which was super exciting. We then headed to the night market to enjoy some local food. We tried snails - which are a local dish; however, we were not overly keen.

Day 2

Day 2

We ended our final day in Cambodia with another great trip.

We visited a local's house where they make silk scarfs, table clothes, ties and handmade requested clothes. They showed us the whole process from how they farm the worms to collect the material from the cocoons, which they then spin by hand and later use to make their items. I couldn’t believe the incredible details in their designs which can take several days to make.
