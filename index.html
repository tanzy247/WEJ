New Zealand - North

Bay of Islands

Bay of Islands

When we first arrived in New Zealand, we picked up our van and headed for the Bay of Islands. We spent a few days travelling around, visiting different beaches, towns and forests.

This forest - the Puketi Forest is filled with native New Zealand trees (Kauri), which are super impressive because of the size of them - they are one of the largest.

Rotorua

Rotorua

After visiting the Bay of Islands, we headed to Rotorua. Famous for its Geothermal activity - including the largest geyser in the southern hemisphere - as well as mud pools and hot springs.

We visited a centre which showcases Mouri culture. This included hand made crafting such as weaving, woodwork and stone sculptures.

Tongariro

Tongariro

We then headed for Tongariro National Park. We set out on our first hike where we got to see beautiful lakes and a volcano.

Wellington

Wellington

Next to Wellington where we walked up Mount Victoria to get views over the city, before boarding the ferry early the next morning to make the journey to the South Island.

New Zealand - South

Abel Tasman

Abel Tasman

Abel Tasman National Park had stunning beaches

Punakaiki

Punakaiki

We visited pancake rocks (named after their mysterious layered formation) and Paparoa National Park.

Franz Josef

Franz Josef

We hiked Robert’s track to get closer views of Franz Josef Glacier.

Franz Josef

This little lake is called Peters Pool and it was formed by ice that was left by the glacier as it reseeded over the years.

Mount Aspiring

Mount Aspiring

Mount Aspiring National Park. We hiked to Brewster hut and then on to Brewster glacier. This was the hardest hike we have ever done but it was so worth it to get so close and even be able to actually touch the glacier!

Wānaka

Wānaka

Then we headed for Wānaka, which has incredible scenery and was fun just to drive through!

Roys Peak

Roys Peak

Hiking Roys Peak. Incredible views but bloody hard work! 3 hours of uphill to reach the peak which sits at 1578m. And an awesome campsite afterwards over looking the mountains. What a way to wake up!

Queenstown

Queenstown

Then we headed for Queenstown for a well deserved day off from hiking. Instead we decided to try out the Luges (a bit like go karts) on top of Bob’s Peak. These were even more fun than we imagined! Whizzing down the mountain trying to overtake each other.

We also saw an amazing piece of art!

Milford Sound

Milford Sound

More scenic drives and some chill time by a lake we made our way to Milford Sound.

En route another hike and an incredible pie!
