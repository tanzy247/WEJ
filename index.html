Brazil

Rio de Janerio

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer was a must see of course! You can see it all over the city. The train ride up was also quite nice through a little forest.

Sugarloaf Mountain

Sugarloaf Mountain

Some fabulous views over the city including the beaches, bays and of course Christ the Redeemer. Rio was actually our favourite city so far because of all of the beautiful landscapes.

Two Brothers Hike

Two Brothers Hike

We also did a short hike up Two Brothers lookout, which was worth it for the views but also stupidly hot! I was very excited to spot some little monkeys on the way up!

Lapa Steps

Lapa Steps

We visited a tourist attraction in the city, which is a large staircase decorated with mosaics in the colours of the Brazil flag.

It was super impressive how the whole staircase had been made up of so many different parts. Intrestingly it was actually created by a Chilean artist called Jorge Selarón.

Boat Trip

Boat Trip

We went on a boat trip around the bay and got to see Sugarloaf mountain and Christ the Redeemer from a distance and then enjoy the sunset.

Ilha Grande

Ilha Grande

We spent 2 days winding down on an island off Rio called Ilha Grande. We visited some incredible beaches and lagoons with gorgeous clear waters. One of my favourite things being seeing turtle swimming whilst we were snorkelling. We also visited a cave.

The Acaiá Cave hides a wonderful spectacle inside. Approximately 8 meters below sea level, there is a crack in the coastal stone, through which seawater has access to an underground hall where there is an underground stone beach. Sunlight propagated through water forms the phenomenon of fluorescence. We were told there are only three caves like it in the world.
