India

Ladakh

Ladakh

Whilst Jade briefly returned home, Mike started the Indian adventure in Ladakh, hiking in the Indian Himalayan mountains. He really enjoyed the peaceful scenery - hiking for 6 days on the Markha Valley Trek.

Delhi

Delhi

After Ladakh, Jade and Mike reunited in Delhi to continue their Indian adventure. Spending time in Delhi exploring the Red Fort, Lotus Temple, Spice Markets and trying different delicacies including chai tea made in a mud cup, which they only use once.

Jaipur

Jaipur

After Delhi, we set off on a train to Jaipur - which certainly showed the chaos of India including cows, monkeys and camels on the street.

We also explored the Pink City and Amber Fort.

Agra

Agra

After Jaipur we hopped on another train heading for Agra. Where The Indian traffic continued.

An early 4am start seemed ridiculous but it was certainly worth it to have tranquil, unspoiled views of the beautiful Taj Mahal before all of the tourists flooded in. We also visited a building referred to as the baby Taj, which the Taj Mahal was actually modelled on.

Whilst in Agra, we visited a marble craft shop where we were able to watch a demonstration of how the decorations inside the Taj Mahal were made - by hand using marble and gemstones. It was extremely impressive to see the process and learn more about this incredible craftsmanship that less than 200 people know how to do. Also all of the stones are collected and shipped especially from all over the world.

Varanasi

Varanasi

After Agra we caught an overnight train to our final Indian destination - Varanasi.

We immersed ourselves into the culture and visited the River Ganges at sunset and sunrise to experience the ceremony where they worship the river. We also got to see cremations - a main reason for people to visit the Ganges as they believe they will return to heaven. Lots of people were also washing in the river to cleanse themselves of sin. It was certainly an interesting experience and we thoroughly enjoyed learning more about a different culture and religion.

On our last day in Indian we enjoyed a cooking class where we learnt how to make a veggie curry and chapatis from scratch.
