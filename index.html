Costa Rica

Monteverde

Day 1

Day 1

Ziplines and a Tarzan swing through the Cloud Forest.

Absolutely epic! Although it rained and we got absolutely soaked.

Day 2

Day 2

We visited the Monteverde Cloud Forest, where we took different trails and walked round for four hours exploring nature.

Day 2

This blew my mind! We happened to be stood at the top of the views of the Cloud Forest. The rain falling on the left flowed to the Pacific and the rain falling on the right flowed to the Caribbean.

La Fortuna

Day 1

Day 1

We hiked up a dormant volcano - Cerro Chatto. It was one of my favourite hikes ever. It really felt like being in the jungle. When we reached the top we climbed down into the crater and swam in the lagoon.

Day 1

After the hike we went to relax at some local hot springs. All of the water is heated naturally by the near by Arenal Volcano.

Day 2

Day 2

We visited a nature walk early in the morning in the hope to spot some wildlife. We were pleased to see sloths (including a mum and her baby), poisonous frogs, lots of birds including toucans, agouti and red squirrels.

Day 2

In the afternoon we visited La Fortuna Waterfall where we had a lovely swim and short hike around the waterfall.

Puerto Viejo

Day 1

Day 1

We visited Cahuita National Park. Again we saw lots of sloths, some howler monkeys, birds, agouti, coaties, lizards and a snake in a tree.

Day 2

Day 2

We biked along the coast and enjoyed a swim in the sea.

Day 3

Day 3

We went on a chocolate making tour and saw more sloths. I can certainly see why it is one of their national animals!

Day 3

This is how the Cacao starts out as tiny little flowers. And on one tree there could be as many as 7,000 but then only around 100 will be pollinated and turn into pods.

Day 3

These are the pods on the trees.

Day 3

Then they change colour when they are ripe - this can take around 5 months from flower to ripe pod which is ready to pick.

Day 3

Inside the pod are the seeds - around 25 - which would make 1 small chocolate bar.

Day 3

The farmer ferments the seeds for 5-10 days and then they are dried in the sun. This takes around 3 weeks.

Day 3

Once they are dried they are roasted and then deshelled using the machinery.

Day 3

Then the beans are mixed until they form liquid chocolate which takes around 3 days. This is where they can add additional flavours.

Day 3

We got to try their 11 different flavours. My favourite was actually one called Machu Picchu including a salt from Peru and one called Tequila shot.

Day 3

Weirdly, these 3 chocolates are exactly the same- just a pure 72% and the only difference is that the farmers ferment their beans for a different amount of time and they actually tasted quite different.

The shop names the chocolate with the name of the farmer so you know which one you prefer.

The local shop we went to was super lovely because it was so small. There was just 5 people who are part of the whole process.
