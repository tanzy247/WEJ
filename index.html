Brisbane

Brisbane

After Cairns we flew back down to Brisbane to make our way south and complete the other half of the east coast. We stayed the weekend with family and had the first home cooked roast since leaving the UK. It made up for our missed Christmas dinner for sure! 10/10

Cape Byron

Cape Byron

This is the easternmost point of the mainland of Australia. We woke up at 5am to watch the sunrise, but sadly it was so cloudy the sun could not be seen!

Dorrigo National Park

Dorrigo National Park

This is a World Heritage listed rainforest.

Nearby we visited a koala hospital where they care for injured koalas from the wild. Here they rehabilitate them before releasing them back into the wild. Unfortunately, sometimes they injuries are too severe and they would not survive in the wild, so instead they are looked after at the hospital.

Crowdy Bay National Park

Crowdy Bay National Park

Here we went for a lovely walk along the coast. Starting at a campground home to wild kangaroos and lizards. During our walk we discovered more chilling in the shade.

Sydney - Day 1

Day 1

We spent our first day in Sydney sightseeing. Visiting the main spots including the Sydney Harbour, Sydney Bridge and the Opera House.

Sydney - Day 2

Day 2

We did the coastal walk from Coggee beach to Bondi Beach. Finishing at Bondi and having a relaxed afternoon.

In the evening we returned to the harbour to see the city at night.

Sydney - Day 3

Day 3

On our first visit to the Blue Mountains, the weather prevented us from enjoying the incredible views. However, we tried again and saw the iconic Three Sisters, as well as taking on the Giant Stairway to the valley below - almost 1,000 steps!

Nothern Territory - Day 1

Day 1

We flew from Sydney to Uluru.

In the afternoon we visited Kata Tjuta - a collection of large domed rock formations. Here we hiked around the rocks and our guide explained how Kata Tjuta and Ayres Rock were formed.

In the evening we headed to Ayers Rock for sunset before sleeping under the stars.

Nothern Territory - Day 2

Day 2

An early start we visited Uluru for sunrise and spent the morning walking around the rock.

The circumference of the rock is around 10km! Our guide took us to some of the caves inside the rock, where he explained how the aboriginal people used to live and hunt within the area.

Lots of the cave paintings tell stories from the aboriginal people.

In the afternoon we headed into the bush, where we had another night sleeping under the stars. We were able to spot the Southern Cross, the North Star, The 7 Sisters and Scorpio.

Nothern Territory - Day 3

Day 3

Another early start as we woke for sunrise at the Kings Canyon. Here we did a 3 hour Hike on the rim of the Canyon.

In the afternoon we headed back to Ayres Rock, where we watched the sunset over Uluru and Kata Tjuda.

Nothern Territory - Day 4

Day 4

We woke for our final sunrise, which was absolutely stunning. Watching the stars disappear as the sun came up. We then visited a camel farm where we were able to feed and stroke the camels.

The singing in Spanish was not a success.
