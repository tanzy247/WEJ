Brazil

Jungle

Day 1

Day 1

Making our way into the jungle. We had to take a taxi to the port in Manaus, followed by a large boat where we crossed from the Black River into the Solimoes River - which is the upper stretches of the Amazon River.

It is not too clear in the photo but the water literally goes from black to brown and they stay completely separated. They donâ€™t mix due to the difference in temperature, velocity and flow.

After the large boat we took a van to a small community and from there we took a canoe up the river to our lodge.

Day 1

Unbelievably, as it is dry season, the small streams off the main river had dried up! So we actually had to hike around 30 minutes from the main river to our lodge. It was unbelievable to think usually they could make this journey by boat. Mike and I saw photos and decided we would love to come back in high season to see it in a completely different light.

That afternoon we went fishing for dinner and caught piranhas and catfish. Billy (our guide) taught us how to kill the piranhas after catching them by crunching their heads! Crazy!

Day 2

Day 2

In the morning, we went on a walk through the jungle looking for wildlife. Luckily, we managed to spot a sloth and even got to hold it - so cute.

Day 2

In the afternoon, we went on another short walk where we spotted monkeys and visited one of the largest trees in the Amazon. It was huge! We then took the canoe up the river to spot caimans, birds and dolphins - grey and pink ones.

Day 3

Day 3

In the morning, we went on a hike into the jungle to learn about the different medicinal plants.

Day 3

This tree was showing that a jaguar had been in the area, because it had sharpened its claws on the bark.

We found the carcass of a caiman, which would have been eaten by a jaguar.

Day 3

They spotted a tarantula nest, and tickled it out so we could see.

Day 3

This was one of the best birds we saw: a tiger heron, which had a stunning pattern on its wings.

Day 3

That afternoon we set up camp in the jungle, which was super cool! We made our own fire. Then salted and roasted fish for dinner. The guide made the table out of wood. It was just crazy!

Day 4

Day 4

The next morning we packed up camp. Billy taught us how to make fans out of palm leaves and we also got to try Brazil nuts.
