Pokhara

After a bumpy bus ride from Kathmandu we arrived in Pokhara and enjoyed a sunset dinner but Phewa Lake.

The next day we heading up Sarangkot Mountain to go paragliding.

The scariest part was having to run off the mountain, but as soon as you were up in the air is was an enjoyable ride with some views of the city.

Unfortunately, the air pollution is currently really bad due to the lack of rain, so there were no views of the Annapurna region. However, we knew we would soon be hiking in the mountains so we didn’t mind.

Day 2

We went on a 12 hour jeep safari hoping to see either elephants, rhinos or tigers. We knew that this is extremely difficult as they are completely wild but we were ecstatic to see a group of elephants as well as a lone male during our day. We were also lucky enough to see a tiger from a far with binoculars, which Mike was super excited about, since big cats are his favourite.

Day 3



We set off at 6:30am for another 12 hour safari, this time walking. By 9am we had received a call from another guide that 2 tigers had been heard at a certain spot so we ran for 30 minutes hoping to reach them in time.

I was worried that we would miss them... however... we spent 7 hours sitting in the same spot, waiting for 2 tigers which we could hear mating in the grass. Time seemed to have slowed down. We were desperate to see them, but it had got to the point we questioned whether we had made the right decision to sit and wait.

Especially when we spent this time sitting in a tree. It certainly wasn’t the most comfortable.

Until BINGO! They crossed the river. INCREDIBLE.

And then something even more remarkable happened. A rhino appeared and chased the tigers.

We sat in the trees watching the rhino bathe in the river hoping to see if the tigers returned...

It was certainly our lucky day! One of tigers came back to the water for a drink. WHAT A DAY!

Waiting 7 hours in 40 degree heat seemed ludicrous but boy was it worth it. As the sun started to set we headed back to our accommodation. We couldn’t believe our luck!

Yoga

After a tiresome few days trekking, we decided to head to a yoga retreat. Hoping to stretch off and relax. We couldn’t have been more wrong!

It felt more like stress positions than yoga!

Everyday we woke to 90 minutes of yoga, followed by 45 minute breath work. In the afternoon we had another 90 minutes of yoga, followed by mediation.

It was an interesting experience and we are glad we went; however, we were pleased when the 3 days were over.

Village

To finish our time in Nepal, we headed to a village in the hills were we spent 3 days staying with a Nepali family. This was an extremely heart warming experience. They welcomed us into their home and looked after us like family. The food was absolutely delicious - cooked with homegrown ingredients and it was really great to see how they lived.

Village

Shout out to Ambica (the wife at the homestay) who goes out to collect grass everyday to feed the cows and carries it back. I couldn’t even pick it up!!

Schools

Whilst at the homestay, we visited three different schools, which was extremely eye opening. The children are extremely well mannered and disciplined - even from as early as 3. I couldn’t believe that they had 3 year olds, standing in line, completing homework and writing in a second language! It was truly inspirational.

Flying to India

Overall Nepal was an incredible country and we finished it off with a free scenic flight to India. On to the next adventures!
