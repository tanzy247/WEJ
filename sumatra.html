Arriving in Bukit Lawang

After a short sleep at the airport we headed to Bukit Lawang. Arriving in Indonesia was such a breath of fresh air. It was so great to be immersed into a different culture again. Just the 4 hour journey from the airport to the village was enjoyable.

I loved seeing all of the bikes weaving in and out, the small shops selling supplies, thatched roof houses, rice fields, children playing football barefoot in the fields. You immediately feel immersed in a different world.

Day 1

We were absolutely spoilt! I was so excited I could have finished there and then and I would have been over the moon. We were so lucky to see 5 orangutans (including a baby), a long tailed macaque and a Thomas leaf monkey.

We also spotted some interesting things along the way including claw marks from sunbears. We even saw an area of rubber trees which are being used by farmers to collect the rubber to export and sell to Europe to make tyres etc.

The animals were definitely a highlight, but the scenery was also absolutely incredible.

Day 1

We trekked for 6 hours in total before arriving at our camp along the river. Here we met our 2 chefs and porters who had already set everything up for us. The food was absolutely incredible and we had a great first evening swimming in the river, playing cards and sharing stories with the guides and porters.

We loved chatting with our team and finding out more about their way of life. The head chef/porter shared his story about how he spent 12 days at sea without food or water until he was rescued by a shipping container. He had been fishing on a boat with 3 others and when the boat broke, they all climbed into the blue plastic box, which they spent 12 days in and ended up in Sri Lanka. Absolutely incredible!

Day 2

We continued our trek along the river and then into the jungle. Again the scenery was absolutely stunning and we managed to spot a black gibbon as well as another orangutan.

We enjoyed lunch by the river and then continued to a new camp. Again in the evening we enjoyed a swim and some games.

Day 3

We walked along the river, which was a nice change of scenery. Unfortunately, we didn’t see any animals today but we did stop along the way to collect banana tree for our curry and to fish for our dinner.

I managed to hold the porter's 70kg bag but I couldn't believe that they walked along the river for hours with this weight - they are truely incredible.

They fish underwater with a spear gun which was really cool to see.

Day 4

We trekked deeper into the jungle and although we didn’t see any animals, we did spot elephant poo and mud on the tree showing that elephants had recently been in the area - which was super cool.

Day 4

In the evening we caught fish by hand as they floated down the river. Our chef also showed us how to carve rocks and we all enjoyed a delicious final dinner together.

Day 5

Instead of making the long journey back from the deep jungle to the village, we rafted back to near where we had started our trek. We decided to go for one final hike in hope of spotting some final wildlife. Again we were super lucky! Spotting more black gibbons, white handed gibbons and a large male orangutan that had last been seen by our guide in August 2023. It was an amazing way to end our 5 day trek!

Day 6

We had an early start from Bukit Lawang. Leaving at 5am on bikes to visit the elephant sanctuary. A place where elephants have been rescued from logging and our now looked after and used for ecotourism.

Being on the back of the bike was absolutely terrifying and exciting at the same time. It was beautiful watching the sun come up and the scenery was fantastic, but there were some seriously sketchy bridges we had to drive over.

I couldn’t believe when our guide turned up with his wife and 2 kids on one bike to collect us. It’s just a completely different way of life.

The elephants were absolutely gorgeous and we loved giving them a scrub and some food.

Sadly, that was the end of Sumatra. It was one of the most amazing places and best trips we had done! I definitely would recommend it to anyone!
